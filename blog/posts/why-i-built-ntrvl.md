---
title: Why I Built Ntrvl
date: April 1, 2026
---

I was about to start a workout when the interval timer I'd been using for months told me I now needed a subscription to keep using it. Same clunky interface. Same limited features. Just a new monthly fee bolted on top.

That was the moment I decided to build my own.

## The problem was simple

I didn't need a fitness platform. I didn't need workout plans or social features or AI-generated routines. I needed a timer that let me set my intervals and get to work. Work for 30 seconds, rest for 10, repeat. That's it.

But every timer app I tried wanted to be more than a timer. Simple functionality was buried under layers of menus and setup screens or they gave you the minimal functionality for free and then locked useful stuff behind a paywall. Quite a few of them ran ads during my workout.

"This should be simpler."

## So I built it

I'm a designer by background, and I've always been drawn to the technical side of things. Ntrvl started as a side project. I wanted to see if I could build the timer I actually wanted to use, one that felt good in your hand and got out of your way the moment you hit start.

The whole thing took about a month in partnership with Claude Code. I designed and built it from scratch for iPhone, with an Apple Watch companion that mirrors your workout in real time. You can set up a session in seconds by dragging to set your times, no typing, no scrolling through number pickers. Haptic feedback, audio cues, and color changes keep you on track without needing to stare at your screen.

I added presets for the workouts I use most (Tabata, HIIT, Boxing, EMOM) and the ability to save your own custom workouts. And then I stopped adding things, because the whole point was to keep this version simple.

## The part I feel strongest about

Today, Ntrvl is free. Not freemium. Not "free with ads." Not "free for the first week." It's free to download and free to use, with no account required and no data collected.

I know that sounds like a sales pitch, but it's actually just a decision I made early on and stuck with. I built this because I wanted a tool that respected my time and attention. Putting ads in it or gating features behind a subscription would have defeated the entire purpose.

My hope, is that if this should grow – and other people love the new features I'm building as much as I do – I can keep Ntrvl free, or as close to free for as long as possible.

## What's next

This is version 1.0, and I already have a list of features I'm excited to build. But I wanted to ship something focused first, something that does one thing well rather than ten things halfway.

If you're someone who just wants a timer that works, I think you'll like it. And if you have ideas for how to make it better, I'm genuinely interested. There's a feedback button in the app that goes straight to my inbox.
